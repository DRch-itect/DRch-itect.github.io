Intentional Classrooms

STRATEGY • 2025 • New York
A workshop designed for educators and backed by neurocience.
VIEW PROJECT

The Question

Every great project begins with a question. In this case, it was: How do we breathe life back into forgotten urban corners? The journey started with endless site visits, conversations with locals, and mapping the invisible networks that make neighborhoods tick.

The Discovery

What emerged was a strategy that went beyond buildings—it was about creating catalysts for community connection. The architecture became secondary to the social architecture we were really designing. People needed gathering spaces, not just buildings.

The Impact

Six months post-implementation, foot traffic increased 340%, local businesses reported 60% higher engagement, and the community successfully organized three major cultural events. The space didn't just change—it came alive.

Industrial Memory

ADAPTIVE REUSE • 2024 • CHICAGO
An investigation into how industrial structures can be reimagined for contemporary use while preserving their essential character and contributing to neighborhood identity through material storytelling.
VIEW PROJECT

Reading the Bones

Industrial buildings hold stories in their bones—decades of work, production, and human activity. The challenge wasn't just about structural adaptation, but about honoring that history while creating space for new narratives to unfold.

Material Conversations

We let the existing materials guide us. Exposed brick talked to new steel. Original timber beams supported contemporary glass insertions. Each intervention was a dialogue between past and future, mediated through careful material choices.

Democratic Space

PUBLIC STRATEGY • 2023 • NEW YORK
A strategic framework for creating more inclusive and accessible public spaces that respond to diverse community needs while addressing climate challenges and social equity simultaneously.
VIEW PROJECT

Democracy in Physical Form

Public space is democracy in physical form. This project emerged from observing how different communities use shared spaces differently, and how design can either include or exclude with seemingly innocent choices.

Beyond Aesthetics

The strategy we developed goes beyond aesthetic considerations to address equity, accessibility, and environmental resilience. It's being implemented across multiple sites, creating a network of truly public spaces that serve everyone.
